# Studio voice — copy guide

A reference for anyone (human or AI) writing for Ingenious Hub.

## The studio's position
A strategic branding and digital-experience studio. We design the brands people choose.

## Tone
Sharp, specific, opinionated. We write like editors, not marketers.

## House rules

1. **Specific over abstract.**
   - ✗ "We transform digital experiences."
   - ✓ "We rebuilt admissions for Allegiance and lifted enrolments 62%."

2. **Numbers when honest, none when not.** Never invent metrics. If a result isn't measured, describe the change in plain English ("ran across three cities without a redesign").

3. **One thesis per page.** Every page argues something. The homepage argues *brands are won on coherence, not budget*. The work page argues *strategy is upstream of design*. Every page ends with a clear next move.

4. **Sentences a thinking reader would underline.** A line should feel like it could appear in a Monocle column or a Pentagram case study, not on a SaaS landing page.

5. **Use punctuation like a designer.** Em dashes for asides. Italics for emphasis (one per paragraph max). Title case sparingly. No exclamation marks.

## Words we do not use

| Avoid | Why |
| --- | --- |
| "elevate", "unlock", "redefine", "leverage" | Filler. Doesn't argue anything. |
| "ecosystem", "synergy", "innovate" | Corporate noise. |
| "cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "next-generation" | Empty superlatives. |
| "transform your brand" | We *design* brands. Transformation is a state, not a verb. |
| "we help X" (without saying how) | "Help" without specifics is filler. |
| "premium" (to describe ourselves) | The work proves it. Saying it doesn't. |

## Words we like

- "design", "argue", "ship", "compose", "publish", "stage"
- "argument", "thesis", "position", "decision", "edge"
- "studio", "practice", "work", "essay" (over "blog post")
- "founder", "operator", "team", "client" (over "partner")

## Sentence rhythm

- Vary length. Long, considered sentences should sit next to short, decisive ones.
- Don't start consecutive sentences with the same word.
- Read every paragraph aloud. If you stumble, edit.

## Examples — before / after

**Hero**
- ✗ "Elevating brands with cutting-edge design and digital innovation."
- ✓ "We design the brands people choose."

**About**
- ✗ "We are a creative agency passionate about helping startups grow."
- ✓ "We're a small senior studio in Jaipur. We argue that brands are won on coherence, not budget — and then we build for that."

**Case study impact**
- ✗ "Achieved significant growth across all KPIs."
- ✓ "62% more admissions in the next intake. Same faculty, sharper brand."

**Contact**
- ✗ "Let's transform your digital presence!"
- ✓ "Tell us where you're stuck. We'll come back within four working hours."

## When in doubt

Ask: *would a thoughtful founder underline this line?* If no, cut it.
